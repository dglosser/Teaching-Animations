Why the slope changes. A tie-rod loaded in tension is stiffest-per-mass when you maximize E/ρ (slope 1). A beam in bending rewards thickness, so the right index is √E/ρ (slope 2); a plate, ∛E/ρ (slope 3). Same chart, different winners — that's why "best material" only means anything once you name the job. (E in GPa, ρ in g/cm³.)
