Why depth grows as √(Dt)

A diffusing atom is doing a random walk · ENGR 170

Each atom takes random jumps, so it does not march in a straight line — it wanders. After N jumps its typical net displacement is only √N, not N. That is exactly why diffusion depth scales as √(Dt) instead of linearly with time.

2-D fixed-length random walk. "Mean net displacement" is the root-mean-square straight-line distance from the origin, averaged over all walkers. Physical mapping: N ∝ t (jumps accumulate with time), so RMS distance ∝ √t, i.e. x ∝ √(Dt).
