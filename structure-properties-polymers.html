Structure → Properties for Polymers

Four structural levers, three properties · ENGR 170

Most structural levers push the same way: more molecular weight, more crystallinity, more crosslinking → stiffer, stronger, more heat-resistant. Branching is the exception — it loosens packing and pushes every property back down.

Structural levers

Molecular weightmoderate
↑ more chain entanglement → ↑ stiffness, strength, Tg/Tm
% Crystallinitymoderate
↑ denser, more ordered packing → ↑ all three
Crosslinkingmoderate
↑ ties chains into a network → ↑ stiffness, strength; suppresses melting

Heavily crosslinked → it does not melt or flow (thermoset). Past this point more crosslinking doesn't raise Tm — it removes melting altogether; the polymer degrades before it flows.
Branching loosens packing → softer, less dense. Notice it drives all three indicators the opposite way from the other levers.
Encoded directions — MW ↑ · crystallinity ↑ · crosslinking ↑ · branching ↓
Directional teaching model: it shows which way each lever pushes and how they combine, using a simple qualitative sum. It is not a calibrated or predictive property model, and the bars are deliberately unitless — no GPa or °C values are implied.
