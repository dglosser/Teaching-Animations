Structure → the whole curve → every property

Two structural roots reshape one curve — and set all six mechanical properties · ENGR 170

Mechanical properties aren't six independent facts. They fall out of two structural roots: how stiff/strong the bonds are, and how easily dislocations move. Drag the two sliders — watch the curve reshape and every property follow.

Structural roots

Bond stiffness & strength med
the Ch 2 energy well — deeper & narrower = stiffer, stronger, higher Tm
Ease of dislocation motion med
bonding character + microstructure — easy slip = ductile; blocked slip = brittle

Resulting properties

E — stiffness
σ_y — yield
TS — strength
ductility
toughness
hardness
Qualitative teaching model. Bars show which way each property moves as the two structural roots change; they are unitless and not a calibrated prediction. Toughness is computed as the actual area under the generated curve, so the strength–ductility trade-off is real within the model.
