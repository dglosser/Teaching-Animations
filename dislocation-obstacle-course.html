Dislocation obstacle course

Strength = the stress needed to push a dislocation past obstacles · ENGR 170

A dislocation glides easily through a perfect crystal — that's why pure metals are soft. Switch on obstacles and it stalls: now it takes more stress to keep it moving. That extra stress is the added strength. Turn the three obstacles on and off and watch the required stress climb.
applied stress τ: 30
stress needed to move: 10
status: gliding
Schematic. Obstacle "resistance" values are illustrative (each obstacle adds to the stress needed for the line to keep gliding). Real strengthening adds contributions from grain boundaries (Hall–Petch), solute strain fields, and forest dislocations (strain hardening).
