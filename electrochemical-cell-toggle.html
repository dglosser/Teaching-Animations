The corrosion cell — break any leg, stop the rust

Corrosion needs all four legs at once: an anode, a cathode, an electron path through the metal, and an electrolyte. Toggle one off and the current stops — that is exactly how every prevention method works.

Anode (metal to protect)where oxidation & metal loss happen
Cathodewhere reduction consumes the electrons
Electron path (metal)lets electrons travel anode → cathode
Electrolyte (water)lets ions move to complete the circuit
—

Every fix removes a leg. Keep it dry or add inhibitors → no working electrolyte. Paint or coat it → no cathode reaction at the surface. Insulate a dissimilar-metal joint → no electron path. Add a sacrificial zinc anode → it becomes the anode instead, so your steel stops being one.
