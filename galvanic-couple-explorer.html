Galvanic Couple Explorer — who corrodes?

Bolt two different metals together in salt water and one is sacrificed. The more active (more negative electrode potential) becomes the anode and corrodes; the nobler one is protected. Pick a pair and see.

Electrode potential ranks how badly a metal wants to give up electrons. The metal with the more negative value is more active, so it loses that tug-of-war and dissolves. The driving voltage is the gap between the two — a bigger gap means a stronger galvanic cell and faster attack. (This is why you never bolt bare aluminum to copper outdoors.)
