Creep: slow stretch under heat and load

Higher temperature or stress → faster creep, sooner rupture · ENGR 170

At high temperature a part under constant load keeps deforming — slowly, but forever, until it ruptures. Raise the temperature or the stress and the steady-state creep rate climbs (it's diffusion-assisted, so temperature dominates — Arrhenius, like Ch 6). Watch the curve steepen and the rupture time collapse.
