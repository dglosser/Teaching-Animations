Why it peaks: fine coherent particles are the hardest to cut, so dislocations must bow around them. Keep aging and the particles coarsen and spread apart (Ostwald ripening) — dislocations bow through the wider gaps more easily, so strength falls. Over-aging is too much of a good thing.
