What actually drives slip: resolved shear stress

τ_R = σ·cos φ·cos λ · slip starts when τ_R reaches τ_crss · ENGR 170

A tensile pull σ doesn't shear the slip plane head-on — only its resolved component does. Rotate the slip plane and watch τ_R rise and fall: it's largest near 45° and vanishes when the plane is square to or along the load. The grain whose plane is best-oriented yields first.

Single-slip geometry with the slip direction taken in the load–normal plane, so λ = 90°−φ and m = cosφ·cos(90−φ) = sinφ·cosφ (max 0.5 at 45°). A real crystal has several slip systems; the one with the largest m activates first.
